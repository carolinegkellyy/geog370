Homework 6 Part 1: Census Data Choropleth

By Caroline Kelly

I present a SNAP Benefit Usage map across North Carolina counties. SNAPS Benefits are supplemental food assitance for those who fall below a certain income threshold. I'm a Food Studies minor and one of the courses I'm enrolled in this semester is centered around food politics. The importance of supplemental assitance, like SNAP Benefits, was illuminated during the COVID-19 pandemic. I thought a choropleth map sharing the usage of SNAP Benefits in North Carolina would help illuminate the prevalence, thus initiating destigmatization of those who need this additional help.

Homework 6 Part2: Census Data Ratios

The first map is a natural breaks map that shows the difference in total households in a county vs the amount of households in a county that receive SNAP Benefits. Natural breaks are where attributes are divided into classes whose boundaries are set within large differences in data.

Natural Breaks Map

The second map is an map that shows the difference in total households in a county vs the amount of households in a county that receive SNAP Benefits. Equal interval divides attributes into equal size ranges. This method divides attribute values into equal size ranges.

Equal Interval Map

The third map is an equal count map that shows the difference in total households in a county vs the amount of households in a county that receive SNAP Benefits. Equal count groups the same count of features in each of the five classes.

Equal Count Map
